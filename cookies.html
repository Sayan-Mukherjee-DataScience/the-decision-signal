What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work properly, improve user experience, and provide analytical information to site owners.

How We Use Cookies

The Decision Signal uses a minimal set of cookies. We do not use advertising cookies, tracking pixels, or third-party cookies for retargeting. Here is what may be set:

Analytics Cookies (Google Analytics)

If Google Analytics is active on this site, it places cookies that collect anonymised information about how visitors use the site — including which pages are visited, how long visitors stay, and what country they are visiting from. This information is used in aggregate to understand how the site is performing and what content is most useful.

These cookies do not collect personally identifiable information and do not track you across other websites. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

YouTube Embed Cookies

Pages containing embedded YouTube videos may cause YouTube (a Google service) to set cookies on your device when you interact with the video player. These are set by YouTube, not by us, and are subject to Google's Privacy Policy. To avoid these cookies, you can choose not to play embedded videos and instead watch them directly on YouTube.

Form Processing Cookies

If you submit a form on this site via Formspree, that service may set a session cookie as part of spam prevention and form processing. This cookie expires when you close your browser.

What We Do Not Use

  • Advertising or retargeting cookies
  • Third-party tracking for marketing purposes
  • Social media tracking pixels
  • Any cookies that build individual user profiles

We do not serve advertising on this site, and we do not share any cookie data with advertisers.

Managing Cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Most modern browsers allow you to:

  • See what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies from specific sites
  • Block all cookies entirely

Note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of some parts of this site. For more information on managing cookies, visit allaboutcookies.org.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. The date at the top of the page reflects the most recent revision.
